// CLASSIFIED NETWORK //
ALLIES
Trusted agents, institutions, and intellectual partners in the field
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // INDIVIDUAL
SANDRA DAY O'CONNOR
Retired Associate Justice — U.S. Supreme Court
The first woman to serve on the Supreme Court and a lifelong crusader for civic education. O'Connor founded iCivics specifically because she feared Americans were forgetting how their own government worked. Miller agreed. Her jurisprudence on federalism remains required reading in his classroom.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // ORGANIZATION
C-SPAN
Cable-Satellite Public Affairs Network
The unblinking eye of democracy. C-SPAN airs the raw, unedited machinery of government — no commentary, no spin, no agenda. Miller uses their archives extensively. If you want to know what Congress actually looks like, C-SPAN will show you. It's rarely flattering. That's the point.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // ORGANIZATION
NATIONAL ARCHIVES
Washington, D.C. — Repository of American History
He has stood in front of the original Constitution. The original. Under glass, in a vault, with nitrogen gas protecting 235-year-old parchment that still governs 330 million people. The National Archives is not a museum. It is the headquarters of everything Miller teaches.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // INDIVIDUAL
JAMES MADISON
4th President — Father of the Constitution
Miller considers Madison the most dangerous thinker the American republic has ever produced — dangerous to tyrants, specifically. The Federalist No. 51 is Miller's personal manifesto. "Ambition must be made to counteract ambition." He has quoted this more times than he has taken attendance.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // ORGANIZATION
BROOKINGS INSTITUTION
Independent Research Organization — D.C.
One of the most respected nonpartisan think tanks in the world. When Miller wants to assign primary policy analysis to students, Brookings provides the ammunition. Their research on election administration, institutional reform, and democratic backsliding is on his syllabus every semester.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // INDIVIDUAL
THURGOOD MARSHALL
Associate Justice — U.S. Supreme Court
Marshall argued before the Court that segregated schools were unconstitutional — and won. Brown v. Board of Education is arguably the most important Supreme Court decision of the 20th century. Miller teaches Marshall not just as a legal figure, but as proof that one person with a constitutional argument can change the country.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // ORGANIZATION
ACLU
American Civil Liberties Union
Whether you agree with every case they take or not, the ACLU has been arguing for civil liberties in American courts for over a century. Miller assigns their case briefs. Not because he agrees with everything they do — but because they show students how the Bill of Rights works in practice, not just in theory.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // INDIVIDUAL
ALEXANDER HAMILTON
1st Secretary of the Treasury — Federalist Papers Co-Author
The man was the original workaholic. Fifty-one Federalist Papers. A national banking system. An entire financial architecture for a country that didn't know what it was yet. Miller respects the output. He assigns Federalist No. 70 every year without fail. Hamilton was right about executive energy.
◈ CONFIRMED ALLY // ORGANIZATION
iCIVICS
National Civic Education Nonprofit
Founded by Justice O'Connor, iCivics has reached over 8 million students with free, game-based civic education tools. Miller uses their simulations in class. He believes the best way to understand how a bill becomes a law is to try — and fail — to pass one yourself. Students agree. They fail constantly. They learn more.

⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // HISTORICAL FIGURE
KING GEORGE III
Former Monarch — British Empire
The original reason the Bill of Rights exists. Every single amendment in the Bill of Rights was written as a direct response to something George III did to the colonists. Miller uses this framework every year. No Rights exist in a vacuum. Every one was earned by fighting a specific tyranny. George made that tyranny very obvious.
THREAT LEVEL
⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // HISTORICAL FIGURE
JOSEPH McCARTHY
U.S. Senator — Wisconsin (1947–1957)
A case study in how fear can hijack due process. Senator McCarthy weaponized the idea of disloyalty to destroy careers, silence dissent, and intimidate Congress and the press. Miller teaches McCarthyism as one of the most dangerous moments in American civil liberties history — a warning that shows up in every unit on the First and Fifth Amendments.
THREAT LEVEL
⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // CONCEPT
CIVIC APATHY
The Enemy Without a Face — Nationwide
Miller's most persistent and dangerous adversary. It has no headquarters. It doesn't campaign. It just convinces people that voting doesn't matter, that policy is too complicated, that government is someone else's problem. Every year Miller fights it in classroom 214 with primary sources, Socratic questioning, and a dry wit that refuses to let indifference survive the period.
THREAT LEVEL
⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // HISTORICAL FIGURE
AARON BURR
Former Vice President — United States
Shot Alexander Hamilton. Attempted to separate the western territories from the Union in what became known as the Burr Conspiracy. Was tried for treason and acquitted only on a technicality. Miller teaches Burr as proof that ambition without principle doesn't just corrupt individuals — it can fracture republics. Burr remains a cautionary tale with excellent aim.
THREAT LEVEL
⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // INSTITUTIONAL
GERRYMANDERING
Systemic Electoral Manipulation — All 50 States
Named after Elbridge Gerry, whose 1812 redistricting produced a district shaped like a salamander. Miller devotes an entire week to redistricting — how it works, why both parties do it, and why it is one of the most effective ways to subvert representative democracy while following the letter of the law. Students leave that week furious. That's the goal.
THREAT LEVEL
⚠ CONFIRMED THREAT // HISTORICAL FIGURE
RICHARD NIXON
37th President — United States
Watergate. The Saturday Night Massacre. Eighteen and a half minutes of missing tape. Nixon's presidency is Miller's definitive case study in executive overreach, obstruction of justice, and the question of whether a sitting president is above the law. He teaches it with relish. United States v. Nixon is, in his view, the Supreme Court's finest hour.

LOCATIONS
Known operational sites, field visits, and sites of significant constitutional history
// 7 CONFIRMED LOCATIONS
WASHINGTON, D.C.
38.9072° N, 77.0369° W
The Capitol, the Supreme Court, the National Archives. Miller has walked every corridor. He brings students here when he can. When he can't, he brings it to them.
PHILADELPHIA, PA
39.9526° N, 75.1652° W
Independence Hall. The Constitutional Convention of 1787. The room where the Republic was built behind closed doors, in the summer heat, by men who disagreed about almost everything and compromised on the rest.
GETTYSBURG, PA
39.8309° N, 77.2311° W
Lincoln's address here redefined the American project. "A new birth of freedom." Miller quotes it from memory. He believes the Gettysburg Address is the greatest political speech in American history and will argue that point with anyone, anywhere, at any time.
MONTGOMERY, AL
32.3668° N, 86.2999° W
Rosa Parks. The Montgomery Bus Boycott. The Civil Rights Movement's ground zero. Miller teaches the movement not as history but as instruction manual — how ordinary citizens changed the law through organized, nonviolent resistance.
NEW YORK CITY, NY
40.7128° N, 74.0060° W
The first capital of the United States. Home of the Federalist Papers' publication. Where Hamilton built the nation's financial system and where the machinery of American power was first assembled. Also where he was famously replaced by a musical.
LITTLE ROCK, AR
34.7465° N, 92.2896° W
Central High School, 1957. President Eisenhower deployed the 101st Airborne to enforce desegregation. The moment federal power was used to protect civil rights on American soil. Miller says if you don't understand Little Rock, you don't understand the 14th Amendment.
